Pest & Bird Protection

🪶
Birds
  • Hoop frames over raised beds with bird netting — easy to lift for harvesting
  • Individual pot covers for strawberries — net the moment fruit forms
  • Shiny tape or old CDs offer some deterrence but netting is the only reliable method
🐌
Slugs & Snails
  • Wool pellets around seedlings NOW — before they even emerge is ideal timing
  • Copper tape around all pot and planter rims
  • Sharp grit or crushed eggshell around base of plants
  • Hand-pick at dusk with a torch after rain — very effective
  • Avoid straw mulch — creates perfect hiding spots for slugs
  • No slug pellets — harmful to hedgehogs and wildlife
🐛
Aphids
  • Strong blast of water to knock aphids off — simple and effective
  • Neem oil spray — organic, disrupts lifecycle without harming ladybirds
  • Marigolds attract aphid predators — already in trellis planter ✅
  • Nasturtiums as trap crop — aphids prefer them over your veg ✅
  • Brassicas in Raised Bed 2 are aphid magnets — watch closely!

Seasonal Timeline

🌱 March — Now
Fleece over both raised beds · put wool pellets down now · continue chitting potatoes · sow courgette & spinach indoors · keep lettuce seedlings indoors · plant asparagus crowns into Raised Bed 2 centre
🌿 Late March
Sow carrots directly into Grow Bag 2 · buy and plant perennial kale (Daubenton ×2) into Raised Bed 1 centre · check on direct sown seeds — fleece on if cold snap forecast
🌸 April
Harden off lettuce — outside during the day, inside at night · sow pak choi and spring onions · start hardening tomatoes, aubergine, chillies on mild days · watch for slugs as temperatures rise
☀️ Late April / May
Transplant lettuce into Planter 2 · plant tomatoes out into corner trellis planter · plant aubergine and chillies into their pots after last frost · sow purple curly kale per seed packet
🍅 May Onwards
Begin weekly tomato feeds once flowering · earth up potatoes regularly · net strawberries the moment fruit starts forming · watch brassicas closely for cabbage white butterflies · enjoy the garden! 🌿
